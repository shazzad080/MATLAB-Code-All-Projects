Clashes escalate: Iran and Israel locked in tit-for-tat strikesTehran / Jerusalem, June 19, 2025 – The confrontation between Iran and Israel has entered a perilous new stage, as airstrikes and missile barrages intensify, prompting fears of a broader regional conflict.- Israeli air attacks devastate Iran's nuclear infrastructure, including two centrifuge complexes near Tehran, heavily damaging enrichment capabilities, according to the IAEA.- Iran retaliates with missile salvos, launching hundreds of ballistic missiles. Only a fraction reached Israeli urban centers due to the Iron Dome, though the sheer volume has caused civilian injuries and heightened alarm .- Casualty tolls rise sharply. Over 600 fatalities reported in Iran, alongside more than 200 deaths in Israel. Infrastructure—from hospitals to reactor sites—has suffered extensive damage.- Prime Minister Netanyahu signals intent to not only cripple Iran’s nuclear and missile programs, but also provoke potential regime change. - Political shifts in Washington. Former President Trump—though lacking official authority—has reportedly greenlit contingency plans for U.S. military support pending Iran’s next move.- Global powers urge restraint. Russia has cautioned against U.S. intervention, warning of destabilization, while European and UN leaders press for de-escalation and renewed diplomacy.- Covert Mossad strike capability revealed: Prior to air raids, Israeli intelligence allegedly deployed drones to disable missile launchers inside Iran—marking a new hybrid warfare approach.- Humanitarian concerns escalate. Thousands of residents in Tehran have fled southward amid escalating strikes and unofficial evacuation advisories.
